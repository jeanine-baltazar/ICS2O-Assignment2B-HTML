Douglas Engelbart

1925-2013

American Engineer, Inventor, Computer and Internet Pioneer

In 20 or 30 years, you'll be able to hold in your hand as much computing knowledge as exists now in the whole city, or even the whole world.
The Mouse

The mouse was invented by Douglas Engelbart in 1964. The appearance consisted of a wooden shell, circuit board and two metal wheels that came into contact with the surface it was being used on. Engelbart described his invention as an "X-Y position indicator for a display system". In 1972 (8 years later) his partner, Bill English, improved the design by inventing the ball mouse. Using a ball instead of wheels allowed the device to monitor movement in every direction instead of being limited to side-to-side, up and down. In present day, this invention is used by almost everyone to translate their physical motions into the motion of a pointer on a display.

Hypertext

Although Douglas Engelbart was not the "father" of hypertext, he played a role in it's developpement. In 1962, Engelbart started to independently work on his NLS (oN-Line System) at the Stanford Research Institute. Engelbart's NLS system was pioneered hypermedia such as hyperlinked text, diagrams, email, source code, etc. This led to the first demonstration of a hypertext interface to the public at The Mother of All Demos. In 1968, Douglas Engelbart also gave a live online hypermedia demonstration of the work he had accomplished at SRI.
