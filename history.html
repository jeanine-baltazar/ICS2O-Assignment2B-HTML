Douglas Engelbart

1925-2013

American Engineer, Inventor, Computer and Internet Pioneer

In 20 or 30 years, you'll be able to hold in your hand as much computing knowledge as exists now in the whole city, or even the whole world.
Contributions and History

1925

Douglas Engelbart was born on January 30, 1925 in Portland, Oregon.

1942

Douglas Engelbart graduated Portland's Franklin High School.

1948

Douglas Engelbart completed his bachelors degree in electrical engineering at Oregon State University. He was then hired by the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics at the Ames Research Center.

1951

Douglas Engelbart married Ballard Fish in Portola State Park on May 5, 1951.

1953-1955

Douglas Engelbart completed his graduate studies at the University of California, Berkeley. He recieved his masters in electrical engineering in 1953 and completed his Ph.D. in 1955.

1957

Douglas Engelbart took a position at SRI International (Stanford Research Institute) in Menlo Park, California.

1960

Douglas Engelbart founded a lab called ARC (Augmentation Research Center) in SRI and recruited his own team.

Mid 1960's

Douglas Engelbart developed computer interface elements at ARC like bitmapped screens, hypertext, collaborative tools, and precursors to the graphical user
interface. He also created the mouse with Bill English, his lead engineer, and described this wooden shell with two metal wheels as an "X-Y
position indicator for a display system".

1968

Douglas Engelbart showcased the chorded keyboard and many of his other ARC creations at The Mother of All Demos.

2013

Douglas Engelbart passed away at the age of 88 on July 2nd, 2013, in Atherton, California.
